## Platformatic

- Website: https://docs.platformatic.dev
- Founder(s): Matteo Collina
- Location: Italy
- Primary category: Platform Engineering / Internal Developer Platforms

### What they do
Platformatic is a platform engineering framework that helps teams build internal platforms for running backend services and APIs. It focuses on providing standardized runtime components so developers can deploy and operate services without repeatedly solving infrastructure and operational concerns.

### Target user (ICP)
Backend developers, platform teams, and engineering organizations that want to standardize how services are built, deployed, and operated internally.

### Key features
- Runtime framework for building internal platforms  
- Service templates for APIs and backend workloads  
- Built-in integrations for databases and plugins  
- Opinionated defaults for deployment and operations  
- Focus on consistency across services  

### Pricing
Open-source core with commercial offerings (pricing not publicly detailed).

### Positioning
Platformatic positions itself as a foundation for building internal developer platforms, emphasizing consistency, standardization, and reducing repeated infrastructure decisions for backend teams.

### Overlap with Clode
- Both aim to reduce operational complexity for development teams  
- Both focus on the later stages of the SDLC, particularly deployment and operation  
- Both are used by teams looking to standardize workflows and reduce manual effort  

### How Clode is different
- Platformatic focuses on providing a runtime and framework for building platforms, while Clode positions itself as an automation layer that manages and operates cloud infrastructure and deployments directly  
- Platformatic requires teams to design and maintain their platform logic, whereas Clode emphasizes automating ongoing operational tasks with minimal manual intervention  
- Clode extends beyond service runtimes into infrastructure management, operations, and monitoring automation  

### DevRel angle
When discussing this space, Clode can highlight that while platform frameworks help teams standardize how services are built, many organizations still struggle with operating and maintaining those platforms at scale. Clode can be positioned as reducing the operational overhead that remains after platform foundations are in place.
