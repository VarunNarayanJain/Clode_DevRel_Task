## ClearML

- Website: https://clear.ml
- Founder(s): Alexey Yakubovich, Moses Guttmann
- Location: Israel / Global
- Primary category: Other / Extended Categories – AI Integration & Development Assistants

### What they do
ClearML is an MLOps platform that helps teams manage the lifecycle of machine learning projects, including experiment tracking, model training, deployment, and monitoring. It provides tooling to make ML workflows reproducible and easier to operate in production environments.

### Target user (ICP)
Machine learning engineers, data science teams, and organizations building and operating ML systems at scale.

### Key features
- Experiment tracking and reproducibility  
- Model training orchestration and pipeline management  
- Dataset and artifact versioning  
- Model deployment and monitoring support  
- Integration with cloud and on-prem environments  

### Pricing
Open-source core with paid enterprise and hosted offerings.

### Positioning
ClearML positions itself as an end-to-end MLOps platform that helps teams bring structure, visibility, and operational discipline to machine learning workflows.

### Overlap with Clode
- Both operate in the deploy, operate, and monitor stages of the SDLC  
- Both aim to reduce operational complexity through automation  
- Both support teams running production systems in the cloud  

### How Clode is different
- ClearML is focused specifically on machine learning workflows, while Clode targets general application and infrastructure automation  
- ClearML automates and manages ML lifecycle components, but does not manage broader DevOps or cloud infrastructure for non-ML services  
- Clode positions itself as an automation layer across diverse workloads, not limited to AI or data pipelines  

### DevRel angle
When discussing platforms like ClearML, Clode can acknowledge the importance of specialized tooling for ML systems while emphasizing that most organizations run a mix of services, APIs, and data workloads. Clode can be positioned as addressing automation challenges across the wider production environment beyond ML-specific needs.
